# Professional Bio

Giorgia Slongo is a bilingual content quality professional and EFL instructor based in União da Vitória, Paraná, Brazil. She works in English and Portuguese, with experience reviewing student deliverables, creating instructional materials, and building structured documentation. She also works as a data annotator at DataAnnotation, evaluating AI-generated content for accuracy, clarity, tone, and instruction-following.

Her current professional focus is content QA, editorial review, localization, and technical writing. She is drawn to content work where bilingual precision matters — where a wrong word, an ambiguous instruction, or a mistranslated term has real consequences for the reader — and brings both a language teacher's eye for audience fit and an annotator's habit of anchoring judgments to the text itself.

Giorgia's background in language teaching gives her a strong foundation in audience awareness, assessment criteria, grammar, and clear explanation. She applies these skills to content quality work — identifying ambiguity, improving instructional clarity, and adapting language for different audiences and contexts. Her undergraduate thesis (UNESPAR, 2025) investigated the impact of a gamified ESL application on English learning for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder, combining qualitative research methods with applied linguistics and UX analysis.

She is currently completing a Postgraduate Certificate in Editorial Quality Assurance and Text Editing at PUC Minas, building on her Licentiate Degree in Language and Literature (English/Portuguese) from UNESPAR.

She holds a Cambridge B2 First certification and is a Certified TOEIC Test Administrator and Proctor.
